Frocket by Ganz

Lead Game Designer, Programmer, & Producer
December 2012

This is the first game I have designed, developed, and produced using my own game idea. It was made in 3 months with a team of about 5, none of whom had experience creating mobile games. It was created with Unity, and uses several 3rd party services such as Game Center, Lumos, and Apsalar.

The gameplay is similar to that of the barrel shooting mechanic from the Donkey Kong Country series. The major differences being that time is a major element and there many forms of obstacles and puzzles to overcome. I'm very proud of the outcome; a fun, challenging game that looks fantastic.

Available on iPhone/iPad and Web.

Amazing World Friends by Ganz

Lead Game Designer
Fall 2012

I was given a mandate to create a Facebook game using the Amazing World IP. At the time I enjoyed the crafting system used in Castleville and the hidden object gameplay in Gardens of Time. These elements became the focus of the game. I also saw a purpose for "beasties" but didn't like how companies like Zynga had implemented them (animals randomly appear and disable your crops/buildings until you spend energy clicking on them a few times. So another focus of ours was changing that mechanic into a whack-a-mole style of gameplay.

In Amazing World Friends you'll see a nice complete system that involves crafting materials collected by farming, playing hidden object games, and whack-a-moling away your foes. All the while building and decorating a zany homestead for your characters.

It was available on iPad and Facebook, but has since been removed.

dimeRocker by OverInteractive Inc.

Lead Unity & Flash Developer
Spring 2010

The dimeRocker platform allowed developers to create Facebook and MySpace games without learning their respective APIs. Instead developers only needed to know the dimeRocker API, which included achievements, leaderboards, inventories, wallets, and access to social hooks such as wall posts and friends lists. It supported Unity and Flash web games.

We also developed the dimeRocker Arcade, an app on Facebook used as a game portal for Unity and Flash games. Like the initial dimeRocker, we provided achievements, leaderboards, and social hooks. The difference being we displayed the features on the page for you, greatly reducing development time needed to implement UI and logic for achievements and leaderboards. Players had a gamer profile that included recently played games, achievement points, and high scores. The home page worked similar to Kongregate, with categories such as Featured, New, Popular, Hot, and Highest Rated.

The company changed direction in 2011, the dimeRocker platform and arcade are no longer available.

Tire & Brimstone (VFS Final Project)

Unity Developer
Fall 2009

Tire & Brimstone was an online multiplayer vehicular combat game that allowed up to 6 players to compete against (shoot) each other in an arena setting. It was the final group project for my game design course at Vancouver Film School.

I was responsible for the online networking portion of the game. I also created a website and backend logic that allowed us to track player statistics (kills, deaths, wins, losses, etc) and display them in game.
